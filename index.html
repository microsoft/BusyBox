Contributions

  • Introduce BusyBox, a reconfigurable manipulation console with modular electronic and mechanical components enabling combinatorial task generation.
  • Define an affordance generalization benchmark: train on a subset of configurations/tasks and evaluate on unseen panel layouts and interaction types.
  • Provide a teleoperated demonstration dataset covering diverse electronic interaction primitives. [PLACEHOLDER: add counts]
  • Establish standardized evaluation metrics (success rate, first-attempt activation, unseen configuration generalization).
  • Benchmark state-of-the-art VLAs: strong on pick & place / wiping but failure-prone on multi-step electronic affordances.
  • Release (planned) hardware design files, dataset, and evaluation protocol to spur research in affordance-centric generalization.

Results

Headline Finding

Despite high success on conventional pick-and-place and surface wiping, leading VLAs still fail frequently on BusyBox’s electronic interaction tasks, revealing a substantial generalization gap in affordance understanding.
